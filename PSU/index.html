CCRD

CCRD GOAL

To make tertiary education accessible to all through establishment of campuses/extramural studies centers in the different strategic municipalities of the province.

CCRD Objectives:

1. To produce competent graduates in their fields of discipline with skills and capabilities that are consistent with the standards of the main campus;
2. To respond effectively to the skills needed by the local and international labor market;
3. To implement more responsive research and extension activities;
4. To strengthen the collaboration between the Local Government Unit (LGU) and the University in the “educational for all” advocacy.
.

Objectives:

1. Globally competitive graduates in the areas of sustainable aquaculture, responsible capture fisheries, post-harvest fisheries, and aquatic resources and ecology;
2. Fisheries Professionals imbued with high level of integrity, nationalism, and ethical standards;
3. Leaders in their profession and respective communities; and
4. Professionals with knowledge, skills, and positive attitudes in fisheries research, resources management, instruction, extension, and marketing.

Course Offer: Bachelor of Science in Fisheries

Welcome Message

Mayad na adlaw from Palawan State University – Linapacan Campus!

It is with great pride and gratitude that we welcome you to the official portal of our accreditation journey. As the only Palawan State University campus offering the Bachelor of Science in Fisheries program—established in 2010—we remain steadfast in our mission to deliver high-quality, relevant, and transformative education to our learners and the communities we serve

Accreditation is a vital process that strengthens the integrity and effectiveness of our academic offerings. It ensures that our programs meet national and international standards, enhances the credibility of our institution, and reinforces our commitment to continuous improvement. Through this rigorous evaluation, we reaffirm our responsibility to produce competent, ethical, and service-oriented graduates who will contribute significantly to the field of fisheries and to sustainable community development.

On behalf of the PalawanSU–Linapacan Campus family, I extend my warmest greetings to all our stakeholders, partners, and esteemed accreditors. This portal stands as a testament to our dedication, perseverance, and collective pursuit of academic excellence. It reflects our ongoing efforts to uphold a culture of competence, innovation, and integrity. We express our heartfelt appreciation to our University President, our Vice Presidents, our Dean, our Program Head, and to all our devoted faculty and staff. Your leadership, guidance, and unwavering commitment have been instrumental in preparing us for this significant milestone.

We also extend our sincerest gratitude to the Local Government Unit of Linapacan, our supportive barangay officials, and all partner institutions whose collaboration continues to strengthen our campus initiatives. To our students, thank you for your cooperation, participation, and enthusiasm—your growth and success are the very reason for our continuous pursuit of excellence

To all our stakeholders, including parents, alumni, community partners, and friends of the university, your trust, support, and active involvement inspire us to keep moving forward with determination and pride.

Thank you for joining us as we strive to further elevate the programs and services we provide. We look forward to a meaningful, enriching, and successful accreditation process

Mabuhay, and welcome to Palawan State University – Linapacan Campus!

Richard L. Rosal

OIC-Campus Director

Mayad na adlaw from the BS Fisheries Program of Palawan State University – Linapacan Campus!

As Program Head, I am proud to open this accreditation portal, a space that represents not only our documents and accomplishments but also the identity of our program and the values we uphold. For many years, the BS Fisheries program has been shaped by our unique island context—our communities, our coastal resources, and the culture of stewardship that defines Linapacan. This accreditation journey gives us the opportunity to highlight how far we have come and how committed we are to moving even further.

Here, we present the efforts of a small but dedicated team that works every day to improve instruction, research, extension, and community engagement. As we prepare for this evaluation, we reaffirm our commitment to building a program that listens to the needs of our locality and responds with scientific, ethical, and practical solutions. Strengthening fisheries education is our contribution to ensuring that our marine environment and its people continue to thrive.

I am deeply thankful for the support of the University administration—our President, Vice Presidents, and Dean—whose guidance motivates us to maintain high standards. To the faculty and staff of Linapacan Campus, your teamwork and resilience make this program stronger each year.

We also recognize the strong partnership of the Local Government Unit of Linapacan, barangay leaders, fisherfolk, and allied institutions. Your cooperation enriches our students’ learning experiences and reinforces the relevance of our work. And to our students: your curiosity, energy, and determination remind us why continuous improvement is essential.

To our accreditors, thank you for taking the time to evaluate our program. Your insights will help us refine our practices and ensure that our graduates are well-prepared for the evolving challenges of the fisheries sector.

As we begin this phase, we do so with confidence, humility, and hope. May this process reflect not only our readiness but also our dedication to serving our island communities with integrity and excellence.

Welcome to the accreditation portal of the BS Fisheries Program—where progress, commitment, and purpose come together.

Aries Kim Jonson, LFP, M.Sc.

Chairperson, BS Fisheries Program Head
